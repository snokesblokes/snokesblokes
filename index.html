Team Strategy and Policy

We are a war squad. We're good at it because we adhere to a consistent team strategy. To be a Bloke, we require all members to follow said strategy. Teamwork is key to winning wars!

Please see our squad policies and guidelines below.

  1. Base Capability

    Please have your base's defensive capability comparable to its level. Having a level 7 base with a level 5 defense is a liability we can't afford. We'll usually check this when you join and let you know if your base needs work.

    Your attack capability will show itself over time. Feel free to ask questions though, as the team is happy to help you in becoming a formidable attacker.

  2. Participation

    In each war you have 3 attacks. You must use every one of your attacks in each war. If you have doubts on how to use them, ask a squad officer for tactical advice.

    Squad members that aren't using attacks will be removed.

  3. Opting Out

    If you know that you won't be able to participate in a war, let us know. We know you have a life with family, work, school, vacation, etc... and won't always be available. Let an officer know so we can temporarily take you off the war squad. We do this all the time and giving the heads-up is appreciated!

  4. Donations

    Donations are critical in that you're giving them and you're giving the right kind. Squad members will let you know exactly what they need to do their best. Please donate per request and do not donate troops or vehicles that aren't requested. We realize, though, that to fill the request you'll probably need to top the member off with a couple of stormtroopers.

    Sullust donation requests are critical for wars. Squad members will often state exactly what they need on Sullust. Donating other kinds of troops or vehicles is unacceptable and will be grounds for immediate dismissal.

    Even if the squad member doesn't state a specific request for Sullust defence, still be smart with donations. Donate items like speeders, snipers, or probe droids which are particularly great for war defence. Do not give units like dewbacks, shock troopers, or repair droids as that'll assist the enemy in grabbing more uplinks.

  5. Outposts

    Outposts often dictate the tide of war. Pay close attention as this is probably our most detail-oriented war tactic and can make or break the team.

    When a war starts, don't start grabbing outposts. Instead, wait for the opposing squad to take it first. Each outpost (op) starts as a level 5 base. Each time it is captured it will raise a level and become more challenging to capture. Level 9 ops are the most challenging to capture. We let the opposing team take ops first so we attain them at level 9. Here's what we hope will happen for all ops.

    1. Enemy grabs level 5 op which becomes level 6 on capture.
    2. We capture it back and the op becomes level 7.
    3. They take it again and it's now level 8.
    4. We take it, the op is now level 9 and is most difficult for the enemy to capture back.

    As teams start capturing bases, carefully follow these guidelines

    1. If an opposing team has an op, don't attack bases. That's using an attack at a disadvantage. Either get the op back or wait for another squad member to get it back.
    2. Capture bases comparable to your level. If you're a level 6-7, capture the level 6 base. If you're level 8, capture the level 8 bases. Don't force players of higher level to waste attacks on easy targets.
    3. If an enemy gets one or multiple ops, work together to get them back ASAP! Get them back if you can. If not, donate to those who can.

  6. Avoid "Remove Me" Habits

    Sometimes we'll need to remove members that aren't a good fit. These are the reasons for most removals.

    1. Not donating troops to other members.
    2. Not contributing rep points to the squad.
    3. Donating troop types that don't match a specific request.
    4. Donating bad troop types for Sullust (e.g. Shock Troopers, Repair Droids, Dewbacks).
    5. Not following the team strategy (e.g. grabbing level 5 ops and not waiting for an opponent squad to take them first).
    6. Being on the war squad and not using your attacks.
    7. Trolling the channel of being a d*ck.

    Sometimes we have to remove players in bulk when we get a bunch of players that have rushed bases. If that happens we mean not to offend. Having rushed bases on the war squad can cause bad match-ups.

  7. Don't be a D*ck

    The squad is full of nice, groovy people and we have to keep it that way to keep things fun. We refer people to this page so that team strategy is easier to understand, we're winning wars, and we all have fun! Be groovy with us and please don't be a d*ck.
